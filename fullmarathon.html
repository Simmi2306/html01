Full Marathon

A full marathon is a long-distance foot race of 26 miles and 385 yards (42.2 kilometers):
Distance: The official distance is 42.195 kilometers, measured using a calibrated bicycle method.
Course: Marathons are usually run on roads, but can also be run on trails.
Strategy: Runners can complete the marathon by running or alternating between running and walking.
Wheelchair divisions: Some marathons have divisions for wheelchair participants.
World record criteria: To be ratified as a world record, the marathon course must meet certain criteria, including a maximum distance between the start and finish points.
The term "marathon" can also refer to any long-distance race or contest that requires exceptional endurance. For example, a dance marathon or a sales marathon.

Here are some things to know about the full marathon:

History

The marathon was created by French philologist Michel Bréal and was one of the original modern Olympic events in 1896. The distance of the marathon wasn't standardized until 1921. Commemoration
The marathon commemorates the run of Pheidippides, a Greek soldier who ran from the Battle of Marathon to Athens to report the victory.

Popular marathons

Some popular marathons include the New York City Marathon, the London Marathon, the Boston Marathon, the Lisbon Marathon, the Marathon du Médoc, and the Berlin Marathon.

Entry

Entry to some popular marathons is based on a lottery system, but runners who finish a certain time in another marathon are guaranteed entry.
