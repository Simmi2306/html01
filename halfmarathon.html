Half Marathon

A half marathon is a 13.1 mile (21.0975 kilometer) road race that's half the distance of a marathon:
Distance A half marathon is 13.1 miles (21.0975 kilometers) long, which is half the distance of a marathon.
Training A half marathon is a challenging distance, but doesn't require the same level of training as a marathon.
Events Half marathons are often held at the same time as a marathon or 5K race, and may use the same course with a different start or finish time.
Medals Finisher medals for a half marathon may differ from those for a full marathon.
Records The International Association of Athletics Federations (IAAF) recognizes world records for half marathons. The current men's record is 57:31, set by Jacob Kiplimo of Uganda in 2021, and the women's record is 1:04:02, set by Ruth Chepng'etich of Kenya in 2021.
Popularity Participation in half marathons has grown steadily since 2003. Some half marathons may also offer a four-person relay option with a four-hour time limit.

A half marathon is a 13.1-mile (21.1-kilometer) footrace that's half the distance of a marathon. It's a popular challenge for runners who have already completed a 5K or 10K.

Here are some things to know about half marathons:

History

The popularity of the half marathon grew in the 1960s and 1970s, when more races were organized and recorded. The IAAF World Half Marathon Championships was established in 1992 to further cement the half marathon's status as a key event in athletics.

World records

The current men's world record is 57:31, set by Jacob Kiplimo of Uganda in 2021. The women's world record for mixed gender races is 1:02:52, set by Letesenbet Gidey in 2021. The women's world record for women-only races is 1:05:16, set by Peres Jepchirchir of Kenya in 2020.

Training

Strength and conditioning are important for training for a half marathon. These workouts can help improve muscle strength, running efficiency, and reduce the risk of injuries.
